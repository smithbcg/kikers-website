The Long Road

From the dirt up

Hunter's grandfather bought the property in 1972. The early years were hard — at one point the only shredder between Pensacola and Birmingham wouldn't buy the family's crushed cars, and a four-hour haul north didn't pencil out. A farm-commodity crash later pushed the family into bankruptcy.

They came back. In 2001 the family reopened as Kiker's U-Pull-It — Hunter was 13, out in the yard breaking down aluminum wheels. He partnered with his grandfather, learned every corner of the business, and today runs it as the third generation, with his aunt Teresa keeping the books.

"I can let this be a crutch, or it doesn't define me — and we're gonna make the best of it."

Giving Back

Good for the community.

We donate vehicles to the local fire department for extrication training — practice that can save a loved one's life one day. And every car we take in gets recycled the right way: copper and metal recovered, fluids handled properly, good parts going on to keep another family on the road.

End of life giving life. It's not just fluff — it's how we've always done it.
