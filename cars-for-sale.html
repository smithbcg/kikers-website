Straight & Legal

How we sell — honestly

These are project vehicles, sold as-is — no dealer warranty. We'd rather lose a sale than hide a problem, so we show the price, the title status, and the known issues up front, with real photos of the actual car. Bring a mechanic, look it over, ask anything.

Title status matters. Clean = never branded. Rebuilt = was salvage, repaired and re-inspected, road-legal. Salvage = not road-legal as-is; for parts or rebuild. Every listing says which.

Sold AS-IS — no dealer warranty

Once our dealer license is active, every vehicle carries the FTC Buyers Guide window sticker (the "As Is – No Dealer Warranty" version, which Florida permits), with the title brand and odometer disclosed.

That's the law, and it's also just how we do business: by the book.

Reference: FTC Used Car Rule (Buyers Guide).

Questions, Answered

Buying a car here: FAQ

What does "sold as-is" mean? +
It means the vehicle is sold with no dealer warranty — what you see is what you get. We tell you the known issues up front, and you're free to inspect it (or bring a mechanic) before you buy.
What's the difference between clean, rebuilt, and salvage titles? +
Clean = never branded. Rebuilt = was salvage, repaired and re-inspected, and is road-legal. Salvage = not road-legal as-is — for parts or a rebuild project. Every listing states the title clearly.
Can I get it inspected first? +
Yes — come look it over during hours and bring a mechanic if you like. We want you buying with eyes open.
Do you offer financing or warranties? +
No. These are as-is project vehicles, sold for cash (or your own financing). No dealer warranty.
Can you hold a vehicle for me? +
Call 850-435-7630 and ask — availability moves fast, so the sooner you reach out, the better.
