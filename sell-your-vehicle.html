Selling a Car in Florida

With a title — or without one

Most buyers won't explain this. Here's how it works when you sell a junk or end-of-life vehicle to a licensed yard like ours.

📋 If you have the title

Sign it over — we do the rest

  • ✓You sign the title over. Because the vehicle is headed for dismantling, it's processed for a Certificate of Destruction (Form HSMV 82363).
  • ✓Your title record is marked "Cancelled (Junked)" — so it can't come back on you and can never be re-titled.
  • ✓Keep your license plate. In Florida the plate stays with you, the seller.
  • ✓As a licensed Florida salvage dealer, we handle the form and the filing.
Source: FLHSMV Form 82363; Fla. Stat. § 319.30.
🔑 No title? You still have options

Florida's derelict-vehicle pathway

  • ✓When a title isn't available, Florida lets a licensed salvage dealer (that's us) take the vehicle on a Derelict Motor Vehicle Certificate.
  • ✓It applies to vehicles under $1,000 and at least 10 model years old. The form can be signed by the owner or whoever lawfully has the vehicle.
  • ✓There's a short holding period (about 3 business days) before it's dismantled, and it can never be titled again.
  • ✓It's the legal way to handle a no-title junk car — done by the book.
Source: FLHSMV Junk a Vehicle; Form 82137.

Selling your car in Florida: FAQ

How do I sell my car to Kiker's? +
Call 850-435-7630 or fill out the form with your vehicle's year, make, model, and condition. We give you a fair cash offer fast, schedule a same-day or convenient pickup, tow it free, handle the paperwork, and pay you on the spot.
Do you buy cars that don't run? +
Yes. We buy running and non-running vehicles — wrecked, flooded, blown engine or transmission, or just worn out.
Can I sell my car without a title in Florida? +
Often, yes. When a title isn't available, Florida lets a licensed salvage dealer like Kiker's take the vehicle on a Derelict Motor Vehicle Certificate — generally for vehicles worth under $1,000 and at least 10 model years old. Call us and we'll tell you what applies.
Do I keep my license plate when I sell? +
Yes. In Florida the license plate stays with you, the seller, not the vehicle. Pull it before the car leaves.
Will the car still be in my name after I sell it? +
No. For a junked vehicle, the title is processed for a Certificate of Destruction and marked "Cancelled (Junked)," so it's permanently off your name and can't be re-titled.
How much is my car worth? +
It depends on the year, make and model, whether it runs, what parts are still on it, the condition, and current scrap and metal prices. We give you a fair, no-haggle offer for your specific vehicle.
How fast can you pick up my vehicle? +
Often the same day. Or pick a time that works for you. Towing is free across the Pensacola area.
What areas do you serve? +
We buy in Pensacola, Pace, Milton and East Milton, Cantonment, and nearby — across Escambia and Santa Rosa counties.
